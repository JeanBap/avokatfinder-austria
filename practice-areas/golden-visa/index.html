Austria Investor Residence: Aufenthaltstitel & HNWI Tax Options

Austria does not operate a traditional golden visa programme. There is no statutory route granting residence solely in exchange for a real estate or fund investment. Austria's residence permits for non-EEA nationals require an economic activity (employment, self-employment, business establishment) or family reunification.

Austrian Tax Incentives for Incoming Wealthy Residents

Austria does not offer a flat-tax lump sum regime comparable to Italy's EUR 100,000 regime. However: (1) Zuzugsbeguenstigung (EStG 103 Abs 1a) - researchers and artists relocating to Austria may receive a 30% tax exemption for up to 5 years; (2) Austria's Gruppenbesteuerung (KStG 9) and Schachtelprivileg (KStG 10) make it highly competitive for holding company structures - dividends and capital gains from qualifying participations are 100% exempt. Austria imposes no wealth tax and no inheritance tax (abolished in 2008) - key attractions for HNWIs.

Case Study: Singapore Entrepreneur - Austria Startup Route

Ravi, a Singapore tech entrepreneur with EUR 800,000 in liquid assets, sought EU residency to expand his SaaS company into Europe. Austria's Start-Up Founder route was chosen over Italy's Investor Visa (suspended) and Portugal's D8 Digital Nomad visa. Ravi engaged an Austrian Rechtsanwalt to prepare the AWS endorsement application: 40-page business plan, market analysis, employment projections (3 Austrian hires within 18 months). AWS endorsement granted in 6 weeks. Austrian embassy issued the NAG 24a permit in 11 weeks total. Ravi registered the GmbH in Vienna, minimising KoeSt liability through Gruppenbesteuerung with his Singapore parent. Key advantage: no Austrian inheritance tax on EU assets, and no wealth tax on retained company value.
