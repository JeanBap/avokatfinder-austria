Mietvertrag (Lease)

A legal contract establishing the rights and obligations of landlord and tenant regarding property use.

What Is Mietvertrag (Lease)?

A Mietvertrag is a contract between property owner and tenant that grants the right to use and occupy property for a specified period in exchange for rent. These contracts are governed by the Austrian Tenancy Act (Mietgesetz) and Civil Code. Austrian tenancy law provides strong protections to tenants, making written contracts essential.

Austrian law requires written Mietverträge for validity. The contract must specify the property, monthly rent, lease duration, and conditions for use. Key protection provisions mandated by law are often included automatically, such as prohibition of arbitrary rent increases and protections against eviction.

The Austrian Tenancy Act provides extensive rights to tenants, including protection against rent increases beyond legal limits and protection against termination without cause. Landlords must perform necessary repairs and maintain the property in habitable condition. These statutory protections apply to most residential leases regardless of contract terms.

Lease termination in Austria is subject to strict notice requirements and valid cause provisions. For residential tenancies, either party typically must provide 30 days notice, and termination may be limited during certain periods. The tenant has significant protection against arbitrary eviction.

Security deposits and advance rent payments in Austria are regulated by law. Deposits must be kept in a separate account and returned with interest at lease termination, minus legitimate deductions. The law limits deposits to amounts proportional to the monthly rent to protect tenants.

Key Facts

  • Written contract required for validity
  • Must specify property, rent, and lease duration
  • Tenants have strong statutory protections under Mietgesetz
  • Rent increases limited by law except in specific circumstances
  • Security deposits regulated with interest requirements

Common Mistake

Assuming that contract terms override tenant protections in the Austrian Tenancy Act. Many protections are mandatory and cannot be waived by agreement.

Expert Tip

Document the property condition with detailed photographs and an inventory signed by both parties before occupancy. This protects against disputes about deposit deductions for damage.

FAQs

Can an Austrian landlord increase rent arbitrarily?

No, rent increases are limited by the Tenancy Act. Increases cannot exceed the inflation rate or amount specified in the contract.

What notice is required to terminate an Austrian residential lease?

Generally 30 days notice is required, though this may vary. Landlords have additional restrictions on termination without cause.

Are utilities included in rent in Austrian leases?

This varies by agreement. The contract should clearly specify whether utilities are included or paid separately.
