Austria Legal Term

Grunderwerbsteuer

German: Grunderwerbsteuer (Property Transfer Tax)

Austrian property transfer tax of 3.5% of the purchase price (or 2% for transfers within close family). Must be paid before the Grundbuch registration application can be filed.

TL;DRAustrian property transfer tax of 3.5% of the purchase price (or 2% for transfers within close family). Must be paid before the Grundbuch registration application can be filed.

Grunderwerbsteuer (GrESt) is the Austrian property transfer tax, levied on all real estate transfers. The standard rate is 3.5% of the purchase price (or the property's assessed value if higher). For transfers between close relatives (spouses, parents, children, siblings — within the first and second degree of kinship) a reduced rate of 2% applies, calculated on the triple Einheitswert (standardised value) rather than market price, often producing a very low tax bill.

Payment of Grunderwerbsteuer is a prerequisite for Grundbuch registration — the district court will not process a registration application until the tax is confirmed as paid. The notary or lawyer typically handles payment on the buyer's behalf as part of the Treuhandschaft (escrow) process, transferring the tax to the Finanzamt (tax authority) and obtaining the tax clearance certificate (Unbedenklichkeitsbescheinigung) needed for the Grundbuch application.

In addition to Grunderwerbsteuer, buyers pay a Grundbucheintragungsgebühr (land registry fee) of 1.1% of the purchase price for the ownership registration. For simultaneous mortgage registration, the Pfandrechtseintragungsgebühr (mortgage registration fee) is 1.2% of the secured mortgage amount. Together with notary/lawyer fees (typically 1–3%), total acquisition costs in Austria run to 6–10% above the purchase price.

Austria also imposes a Immobilienertragsteuer (property capital gains tax) of 30% on gains from selling real estate, with exemptions for the seller's primary residence (Hauptwohnsitzbefreiung) if they have lived there for at least two of the last five years, and for self-built owner-occupied properties. Sellers who purchased before April 2002 are subject to a reduced tax regime (Alt-Grundstück rules).

For foreign nationals purchasing Austrian property, Grunderwerbsteuer is payable regardless of nationality or residence status. There is no reduced rate or exemption for non-residents. The tax is assessed and collected by the regional Finanzamt, with the notary or lawyer acting as the responsible party for correct calculation and timely payment.

💡 Expert TipIf you are purchasing with family members, explore whether the reduced 2% Grunderwerbsteuer rate for family transfers applies to your situation. For gifts or transfers between qualifying relatives, the tax base is the triple Einheitswert (often 10–20% of market value), making the effective tax rate extremely low compared to a market-price purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Grunderwerbsteuer deductible from Austrian income taxes?

No — it is a capital cost, not a deductible expense. It is added to the acquisition cost of the property for capital gains calculation purposes (reducing your future taxable gain when you sell), but cannot be deducted against income in the year of purchase.

What is the Unbedenklichkeitsbescheinigung?

The Unbedenklichkeitsbescheinigung (tax clearance certificate) is issued by the Finanzamt confirming that Grunderwerbsteuer has been paid for a specific transaction. The district court requires this certificate before processing the Grundbuch registration application — without it, registration is impossible.

Do I pay Grunderwerbsteuer on a new-build purchased from a developer?

Yes — unlike in some other European countries, Austrian new-build purchases from developers are subject to both Grunderwerbsteuer (3.5%) and, if applicable, Umsatzsteuer (VAT at 20%) depending on how the transaction is structured. Some developer sales are VAT-exempt; others have VAT applied to construction value only. Your notary will advise on the applicable tax treatment.
